Couscous: A Practical Guide to Causal Inference with R

urpose of this book

After a few years in my undergraduate economics program, I grew interest in the field of programming. I felt that in the real world, the concepts and theories in the field of economics and finance are brought alive with data. Times are changing, and the ability to code and create elegant computer scripts will be very useful, especially when handling data sets and conducting analysis.

I realized that unless you were on your way towards a computer or data science degree, there wasnâ€™t really a concrete way of getting used to working with data and programming. Additionally, I wanted the content of this book to assist my fellow students in my program.

Therefore, I compiled this book. The purpose of this book is to storehouse all the foundations you need as a fresh beginner, whether you are an undergraduate or postgraduate, to get started with R language programming and data analysis. This book will also dive deeper into the methodologies of causal inference, which will be particularly useful for postgraduate statistical studies and research.

I believe anyone can code, but the questions is how and where do you start?

Couscous aims to be the solution. By the end of reading this book, you will be able to have a grasp of how to use the R effectively.
